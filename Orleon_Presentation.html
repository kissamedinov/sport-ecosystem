Relevance of Automation in Youth Sports

02 / 15

Why does youth sports need dedicated digital automation today?

  • Rapid Industry Growth: Exploding number of private academies, leagues, and training academies.
  • Manual Operations: Reliance on fragmented spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets) for schedule and payment tracking.
  • Communication Fragmentation: Parent-coach coordination is scattered across chat apps (WhatsApp, Telegram), losing critical schedule updates.
📈

The Digitalization Gap

90% of local youth sports clubs rely on manual operations and Excel for managing their schedules.

ORLEON Introduction & Context

Key Limitations of Existing Solutions

03 / 15
  • High Licensing Costs: Enterprise sports CRM solutions are expensive and not adapted for emerging markets.
  • No Unified Player Profiles: History, statistics, team assignments, and attendance logs are kept in disconnected files.
  • Friction in Junior Profile Management: Parents cannot manage their children's team schedules easily from a single, unified account.
  • Lack of Logistics Integration: Field bookings, league updates, and training schedules operate in completely separate applications.
⚠️

Fragmented Workflows

No connection between roster administration, schedule tracking, and tournament brackets.

Goal: Develop a cross-platform ecosystem to digitize the core workflows of youth sports clubs.

ORLEON Proposed Architecture

Technical Stack & Architecture

06 / 15
  • Mobile Client (Frontend): Dart & Flutter. Single codebase, high-performance UI rendering, and reactive state management.
  • REST API (Backend): Python, FastAPI, and SQLAlchemy. Highly concurrent, asynchronous request loops, and strict type validation via Pydantic.
  • Database: PostgreSQL. Solid relational storage with optimized indexing for complex user and match relationships.
  • Security: JWT-based authorization tokens with endpoint-level role verification.
🛠️

Technologies Used

Flutter FastAPI PostgreSQL SQLAlchemy
ORLEON Technical Architecture

Logical Database Schema

07 / 15
  • Complex Relations: Over 15 tables covering clubs, academies, teams, players, parents, matches, and booking transactions.
  • Status Engine: Designed the `team_memberships` link table to manage requests, approvals, and history transitions.
  • Parent-Child Accounts: Child profiles do not require a separate email or login, eliminating user friction. Managed directly under parent records.
🗄️

Relational Integrity

Strict Foreign Key cascading • Optimized query joins for real-time dashboards

ORLEON Backend Infrastructure

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

08 / 15
  • Club Owner: Manages academies, creates team entities, registers fields, and audits finances.
  • Coach: Manages rosters, coordinates training schedules, and inputs match statistics.
  • Parent / Player: Connects to teams, views schedules, accepts invitations, and tracks scores.
  • Field Owner: Creates field configurations, manages rental time-slots, and receives bookings.
